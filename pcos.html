Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS): What You Need to Know

What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder common among people with ovaries, especially during their reproductive years. It occurs when there’s a hormonal imbalance that disrupts ovulation and causes symptoms such as irregular periods, acne, and excess hair growth.

How Common is PCOS?

PCOS affects about 6 to 13% of women globally. However, many remain undiagnosed. For example, around 70% of affected individuals are unaware of their condition. Prevalence varies by region:

  • United States: Approximately 5.2%
  • United Arab Emirates: Up to 27.6% based on NIH criteria

What Causes PCOS?

Although the exact cause is unknown, several factors contribute to PCOS:

  • Hormonal imbalances (elevated androgens)
  • Insulin resistance
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Chronic low-grade inflammation

What Are the Symptoms?

PCOS symptoms can vary, but common ones include:

  • Irregular or absent periods
  • Excess facial/body hair (hirsutism)
  • Acne or oily skin
  • Weight gain or difficulty losing weight
  • Thinning scalp hair
  • Dark patches of skin
  • Cysts on ovaries
  • Fertility challenges

How is PCOS Diagnosed?

Doctors use the Rotterdam criteria. Diagnosis requires at least two of the following:

  • Irregular or missed periods
  • Signs of high androgen levels
  • Polycystic ovaries seen on ultrasound

Long-Term Health Risks

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Heart disease and stroke
  • Endometrial cancer
  • Obstructive sleep apnea

Mental Health Implications

  • Higher risk of depression and anxiety
  • Increased risk of eating disorders
  • Body image issues due to physical symptoms

How is PCOS Treated?

  • Lifestyle changes (diet, exercise)
  • Medications (birth control, metformin, anti-androgens)
  • Fertility treatments
  • Mental health support

Emerging Treatments and Therapies

  • GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., Ozempic, Mounjaro)
  • DPP-4 inhibitors (e.g., Sitagliptin)
  • Supplements (inositol, vitamin D, zinc, spearmint)

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Healthy eating and physical activity
  • Weight loss of just 5–10% can improve symptoms

Importance of Early Diagnosis and Comprehensive Care

  • Early screening helps manage long-term risks
  • Mental health care enhances quality of life

Tips for Living with PCOS

  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle
  • Track menstrual cycles and symptoms
  • Stay in regular contact with healthcare providers
  • Seek support for emotional wellness

Together Through It All: The Story of Mr. Journey and Mrs. Faith

In the quiet town of Sta. Rita West, Aringay, La Union, lives a couple who show us what true love and teamwork really mean. Mr. Journey is a 29-year-old company driver. He and his wife, Mrs. Faith, 28, have been married for eight years. They are both Roman Catholic and have a one-year-old child. They live a simple life, but their journey has not always been easy. One of the biggest challenges they’ve faced is Mrs. Faith’s struggle with polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS), a health condition that can make it hard for women to get pregnant. Even though there’s no history of PCOS in her family, Mrs. Faith has been dealing with it for some time. It hasn’t been easy for her. She said, “I feel emotional when I see pregnant women. It makes me feel stressed.” Going to the OB-Gyne clinic is also hard, especially when she sees other women who are expecting. It reminds her of her own hopes and dreams. But she is not alone in this journey. “Yes, my husband and I always help and support each other,” Mrs. Faith said. She and Mr. Journey believe that being close doesn’t just mean being together physically—it also means being open with each other and sharing their feelings. Mr. Journey feels her pain too. “It’s hard to see my wife suffering. I want her to know that I’m always here, that I won’t leave her,” he shared. Even though he’s the husband, he admits that there are times he feels weak too. “I also feel down sometimes, especially as a man,” he said. This honesty shows how much he cares. Instead of hiding his feelings, he chooses to be open with his wife. Their relationship is built on trust, understanding, and deep emotional connection. For them, simple moments—like a hug before work or kind words at the end of a long day—mean a lot. These small acts of love help them stay strong together. Their story shows that dealing with PCOS is not just the wife’s burden. It’s something the couple faces as one. They support each other through the hard days, and they celebrate the small victories together. Mr. Journey and Mrs. Faith remind us that love is not just about happy times. It’s also about standing by each other through pain and struggle. And in that shared journey, their bond grows even stronger.

Holding on to Hope: Mr. Hope and Mrs. Grace’s Journey Through PCOS

In Duplas, Pugo, La Union, lives a couple whose story is filled with struggle, faith, and the power of never giving up. Mr. Hope, a 26-year-old businessman from Agoo, La Union, has been married for eight years to his wife, Mrs. Grace. She is also 26, a college graduate, a businesswoman, and originally from Balanga, Bataan. They now live together with their three-year-old son, building their life side by side. But before their little boy came into the picture, their road to parenthood was long and emotional. Mrs. Grace was diagnosed with polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS) in 2019. She had no family history of the condition, which made the diagnosis even more unexpected. She shared, “Sobrang lagas ng buhok ko noon at ang bigat ko rin—galing akong 81 kilos tapos naging 48 kilos. Noong 2019 ako pumayat, kaya ako nabuntis. May acne din ako at may hormonal imbalance, sabi ng OB ko.” These physical changes didn’t just affect her health—they also took a toll on her confidence. Like many women with PCOS, she struggled with how she saw herself, feeling unsure and less feminine at times. Despite all this, Mr. Hope stood by her every step of the way. For two years, the couple tried to have a baby. “Sobrang dami naming PT nagamit,” Mrs. Grace recalled with a bittersweet smile. Pregnancy tests became a regular part of their life—each negative result a painful reminder of what they were waiting and hoping for. “Every time na makikita mo na negative, sobrang disappointing,” she said. “Pero at the same time, may hope pa rin, kasi hihilingin mo talaga sa taas na mag-positive siya someday.” Their faith carried them through the hard days. They never lost hope, even when the odds felt heavy. Mrs. Grace took care of her body the best she could. She took prescribed pills, but also added inositol, fish oil, and protein supplements to help her health. Bit by bit, her condition improved—and in 2021, the miracle they had been praying for finally happened. She got pregnant. Their son is not just a blessing, but a symbol of strength, patience, and love. Mr. Hope and Mrs. Grace’s story shows us how powerful it is to keep believing even when things are uncertain. Through PCOS, through waiting, and through countless disappointments, they never stopped supporting each other. And in the end, their patience was rewarded. For couples going through the same battle, their story is a reminder: it’s okay to feel tired, it’s okay to cry—but never stop hoping.

Rooted in Love: The Quiet Strength of Mr. Phoenix and Mrs. Iris

In Udaio, Rosario, La Union, where the sun rises over wide fields and life moves at a steady pace, lives a young couple learning how to grow not just crops—but a life full of love, hope, and strength. Mr. Phoenix, 27, a farmer and Roman Catholic, starts each day early. His hands are rough from work, but his heart is soft for the people he loves most—his wife, Mrs. Iris, and their baby daughter. Mrs. Iris, just 23 years old, a housewife, a mother, and a woman quietly facing one of the challenges of her life. The couple has been married for five years. Life hasn’t always been easy, but together they’ve built a strong foundation. In December 2022, Mrs. Iris was diagnosed with polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS)—a condition that causes hormonal imbalance and can affect a woman’s ability to conceive or manage her health. For a young mother, it was a heavy burden to carry. At first, the diagnosis brought confusion and quiet fear. PCOS came with symptoms she didn’t fully understand—changes in her cycle, discomfort, mood swings. She wondered what this would mean for her, for their growing family, and for her future. But even in those uncertain moments, one thing was clear: she was not facing this alone. Mr. Phoenix, though quiet and hardworking, showed his love in steady ways. Whether it was bringing home healthy food, doing extra farm work so his wife could rest, or gently cradling their baby so she could breathe and take a break—his presence was a constant comfort. They didn’t have all the answers. There were days filled with worry, especially as they learned how to care for a newborn while managing Mrs. Iris’s condition. But they held onto each other, drawing strength from the life they were building and the daughter who brought joy to their home. Love for them wasn’t about big words or dramatic gestures—it was about being there. It was in the quiet prayers at night, in the laughter of a baby learning to smile, and in the look, they shared when the day’s work was done. Mrs. Iris continues her journey with PCOS—taking care of her body, learning more about her health, and adjusting to life as a young mother. But she faces each day with courage, knowing she is supported. Mr. Phoenix may not say much, but his actions speak volumes. He is her partner, her strength, and her home Their story is one of quiet bravery and deep connection. In the fields of La Union, love doesn’t need to shout. It grows slowly, steadily, like the crops Mr. Phoenix plants—nourished by patience, faith, and the everyday miracles of family.

Together in Faith: The Story of Mr. Light and Mrs. Bloom

In the heart of Poblacion East, Rosario, La Union, lives a couple whose love and faith help them face one of the hardest chapters in their life together. Mr. Light, a 33-year-old electrician, lives with his wife, Mrs. Bloom, and their two-year-old child. They’ve been married for six years, and their bond has grown stronger through life’s ups and downs. In May 2022, Mrs. Bloom was diagnosed with Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome (PCOS)—a condition that affects a woman’s hormones, body, and fertility. For her, it was more than just a health issue. It affected how she saw herself. She had acne, hair loss, and hormonal imbalance. She often felt ashamed of her body and afraid of being judged. But she was never alone. Mr. Light stood by her from the start. “Hindi lang siya ang lumaban,” he said. “Ako rin.” He gave his full support—physically, emotionally, and spiritually. Even if he didn’t feel the same physical changes, he felt her stress and pain in his heart. He made it his goal to stay strong and positive for her, showing her that she didn’t have to go through it alone. Mrs. Bloom admitted that there were days when she felt tired and discouraged, especially when it seemed like nothing was changing. But her husband’s love reminded her that she was still beautiful and worthy. With his support, she started to slowly gain back her confidence. They both committed to a healthier lifestyle—taking vitamins, eating better, and staying active. Mrs. Bloom even tried herbal remedies like boiled bayabas (guava) leaves to help clean her body naturally. Prayer became a big part of their journey. “Tuwing nagdadasal kami ng asawa ko sa altar, lagi naming sinasama sa dasal ang anak na hinihintay namin,” Mrs. Bloom shared. Their faith gave them strength. Even in the most painful moments, they never gave up. Mr. Light remembered how hard it was when they first found out about her PCOS. “Hindi ko alam kung anong gagawin,” he said. “Pero alam ko, kailangan niya ako.” From then on, he made sure she knew he was always there. They both believe that one day, God will bless them again. But for now, they hold on to what matters—faith, love, and each other. Their story is a reminder that real love shows up in the hardest times. It listens, supports, and believes—even when the future feels uncertain. For Mr. Light and Mrs. Bloom, the journey isn’t over, but they walk it together, holding on to faith and hope with every step.
